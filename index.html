01 — Signal

What I'm listening for

I split my time between hospital hallways and research notebooks — shadowing physicians in the morning, then spending the evening asking whether a model could have caught what took a clinician years of pattern recognition to see.

I don't think AI belongs at the center of medicine. I think it belongs just behind the doctor's shoulder — quiet, fast, and only speaking up when it has something worth saying.

This site is where I keep the throughline between the two: the pulse that runs from a line of code to a patient's chart.

02 — Focus

Three places I want AI to show up in medicine

01 / Diagnostics

Notice what's easy to miss

Second-reader systems that flag the faint signal in an image or chart — not to replace a clinician's eye, but to make sure it never has an off day.

02 / Human-centered care

Give time back to the room

Ambient tools that handle the notes and the admin, so the physician can actually look at the patient instead of the screen between them.

03 / Predictive medicine

Catch the quiet years

Models that notice risk building up long before symptoms do, turning medicine from reactive to preventive.
